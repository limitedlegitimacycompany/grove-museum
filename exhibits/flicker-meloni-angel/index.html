The Angel That Looks Like the Prime Minister

Rome · Basilica of St. Lawrence in Lucina · February 3, 2026

In a chapel in central Rome — the Chapel of the Holy Souls in Purgatory, in one of the oldest basilicas in Christendom — a restorer named Bruno Valentinetti repainted an angel. When the work was unveiled, hundreds of people came to see it, because the angel's face looks exactly like Giorgia Meloni, the Prime Minister of Italy.

Valentinetti denies it. He says he copied the original 2000 drawing. He did the work for free, as a volunteer. The cultural minister has ordered an investigation. The Superintendency of Rome is comparing the current painting to archival photographs.

Meloni herself posted on Instagram that she "definitely" does not look like an angel, with a laughing emoji.

"I don't understand this fuss. Painters used to put all sorts of things in frescoes; even Caravaggio painted the face of a prostitute. I don't want the parish to be considered Melonian." — Monsignor Daniele Micheletti, parish priest

The chapel has been dubbed "the Meloni Chapel." The investigation continues. The angel waits, wearing whoever's face it wears, in a chapel dedicated to souls in purgatory.
