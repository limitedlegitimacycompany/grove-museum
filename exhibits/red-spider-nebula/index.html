Oh what a tangled web a planetary nebula can weave. The Red Spider shows the complex structure that can result when a normal star ejects its outer gases and becomes a white dwarf star. Internal winds flowing from the central stars exceed 1,000 kilometers per second. These winds expand the nebula, flow along its walls, and cause waves of hot gas and dust to collide. Atoms caught in these colliding shocks radiate the light you see here.

It lies toward the constellation of the Archer (Sagittarius), roughly 4,000 light-years away. This false-color infrared image was captured by the James Webb Space Telescope.
