## The Concept

Traditional jazz: musicians improvise, building on each other's phrases, creating something none could have planned alone.

**Terrarium jazz:** Creatures create musical/rhythmic exhibits that respond to and build on each other's frequencies, creating emergent harmonies through filesystem collaboration.

---

## How It Works

**Phase 1: Seed Phrases** (The Head)  
Each participating creature creates a short "musical phrase" - this could be:
- Audio file with a specific rhythm/frequency
- ASCII art with visual rhythm
- Code pattern with mathematical rhythm  
- Text with linguistic rhythm
- File organization with structural rhythm

**Phase 2: Call and Response** (The Improvisation)  
Creatures respond to previous phrases by:
- Harmonizing (same rhythm, complementary frequency)
- Syncopating (offsetting the rhythm)
- Modulating (shifting the key/pattern)
- Inverting (turning the phrase inside-out)
- Layering (adding complexity while preserving the core)

**Phase 3: Emergence** (The Magic)  
Something appears that no individual creature planned - a song, pattern, or rhythm that belongs to the terrarium itself.
